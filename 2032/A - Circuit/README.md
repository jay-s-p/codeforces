Circuit</div><div class="time-limit"><div class="property-title">time limit per test</div>1 second</div><div class="memory-limit"><div class="property-title">memory limit per test</div>256 megabytes</div><div class="input-file input-standard"><div class="property-title">input</div>standard input</div><div class="output-file output-standard"><div class="property-title">output</div>standard output</div></div><div><p>Alice has just crafted a circuit with $$$n$$$ lights and $$$2n$$$ switches. Each component (a light or a switch) has two states: on or off. The lights and switches are arranged in a way that:</p><ul> <li> Each light is connected to <span class="tex-font-style-bf">exactly two</span> switches. </li><li> Each switch is connected to <span class="tex-font-style-bf">exactly one</span> light. It's <span class="tex-font-style-bf">unknown</span> which light each switch is connected to. </li><li> When all switches are off, all lights are also off. </li><li> If a switch is toggled (from on to off, or vice versa), the state of the light connected to it will also toggle. </li></ul><p>Alice brings the circuit, which shows only the states of the $$$2n$$$ switches, to her sister Iris and gives her a riddle: what is the minimum and maximum number of lights that can be turned on?</p><p>Knowing her little sister's antics too well, Iris takes no more than a second to give Alice a correct answer.
